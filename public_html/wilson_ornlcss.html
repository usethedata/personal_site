Current Projects and Roles
  • Enterprise Architect, Oak Ridge National Laboratory: My primary focus is on identity and access management, remote access tools and infrastructure, enabling data-intensive science (particularly in the context of cloud computing), mobile devices, enabling Bring Your Own Device, and enabling scientific collaboration.
  • Core Cyberinfrastructure Team and Leadership Team Member, Data Observation Network for Earth (DataONE): DataONE is a largely NSF-supported project working ensure the preservation, access, use and reuse of multi-scale, multi-discipline, and multi-national Earth observational data through a broad education and outreach program, a sound understanding of sociocultural factors, and a cyberinfrastructure informed by those sociocultural factors and built on established tools.
  • Advisory Committee Member USA National Phenology Network: Phenology is the study of periodic plant and animal life cycle events and how these are influenced by seasonal and interannual variations in climate. Examples include the timing of leafing and flowering, agricultural crop stages, insect emergence, and animal migration. All of these events are sensitive measures of climatic variation and change, are relatively simple to record and understand, and are vital to both the scientific and public interest.  USA NPN is an emerging network which seeks to collect and combine phenological data from a variety of sources, to provide continental scale data on the changes in ecosystems.
  • Adjunct Professor of Information Sciences, University of Tennessee: As part of the Center for Information and Communication Studies and the School of Information Sciences, I'm interested in the education of data and information professionals, particularly in terms of the design, cybersecurity, and usability of information systems and digital archives.
 
Research Interests
  • Environmental and Ecological Informatics, particularly the storage, retrieval, and analysis of data associated with global climate issues.  I'm particularly interested in the data architecture and systems architecture aspects of environmental and ecological informatics problems.
  • Cybersecurity and cyberinfrastructure in open research:  How can we ensure the integrity of our systems and protect the privacy of our users while still providing open access to data?  What's the best way to structure the cyberinfrastructure for a given set of research problems?
  • Phenology: the study of the timings of annual events in plants and animals.
  • Multivariate data analysis, particularly the application of latent variable methods (e.g. Principal Components Regression and Partial Least Squares regression) to scientific problems.
  • The social aspects of data use and re-use.  In particular, I'm interested in ways of encouraging researchers to share their data, ways of creating better cite-ability for datasets, and ways of training students in the creation and maintenance of metadata.
 
Employment History
  • Oak Ridge National Laboratory (2006-present; Oak Ridge, TN) I started as the Systems Engineer for the NASA-funded ORNL Distributed Active Archive Center for Biogeochemical Dynamics (ORNL DAAC). I subsequently became the manager for the ORNL DAAC and the Group Leader for the Environmental Data Science and Systems group. During this time, I also became an Adjunct Professor at the University of Tennessee's School of Information Sciences, as part of their emphasis on scientific information systems. In 2010, I moved to ORNL's Information Technology Services Division as the Group Leader for Client and Collaboration Systems. In 2012, I moved back to a more technical role as an Enterprise Architect, with a particular focus of helping enable the research mission of ORNL.
  • The Dow Chemical Company (2001-2006; Midland, MI) At Dow, I was a Technical Leader in the Research Information Systems organization, primarily working on implementing informatics and analytics tools to support high throughput research in catalysis, material sciences, and formulations.
  • Dow Corning Corporation (2000-2001; Midland, MI) As part of the Chemometrics and Data Fusion Group, I designed, implemented, and used an information system to collect data from a highly instrumented pilot plant to improve the production of industrial sealants.
  • Eastman Chemical Company (1988-2001; Kingsport, TN) I started in the Fundamental and Exploratory Analysis group working on a variety of large-scale data analysis projects to better understand several of Eastman's chemical production processes, including cellulose acetate, polyethylene terephthalate, and coal gasification. I helped design a novel Raman spectrometer for on-line applications and tools for laboratory-scale polymer production. I moved to being a polymer chemist, researching new materials, particularly ones with improved barrier and strength properties. That led to my early work in high throughput methods for materials, which led to a role in chemical information management, and then leading a small team doing computational chemistry.
